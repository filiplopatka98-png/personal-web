WordPress has a reputation for being a slow CMS. Deserved? Partly — but after five years of tuning WP sites I can say it can be fast, you just have to know what you're doing.

I build custom themes from scratch. No Elementor, Divi, or WPBakery. These page builders generate redundant HTML/CSS/JS, make the site heavy, and hard to maintain after a year or two. Instead, I use a Gutenberg block library tailored to the client's specific workflow — pre-built blocks for product categories, campaign pages, blog layouts.

Once handed over, the client can edit the site themselves. No "you have to call the developer" to change text on the homepage. I prepare documentation during the project, and the hand-off call resolves any remaining questions.

WooCommerce when an e-shop is needed — I can set up payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, GoPay), VAT settings for the EU, multilingual setup via Polylang or WPML. I optimize performance through WP Rocket + ShortPixel + custom critical CSS. Lighthouse 90+ is the baseline, not a bonus.
