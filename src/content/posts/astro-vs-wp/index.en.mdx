Clients ask me: "Should I build on Astro or WordPress?" Wrong question — like asking whether a car or a bicycle will get you there. Depends on the goal.

## Go WordPress if...

- The client edits content daily / weekly (admin panel is their natural habitat)
- Site has an e-shop with more than 100 products (WooCommerce ecosystem is unbeatable)
- The team has an in-house "webmaster" with WP background
- Budget < €5,000 and scope is "classic company site + blog"

## Go Astro if...

- Marketing/landing/docs site where performance score (Lighthouse 95+) is a deal-breaker
- Content is primarily markdown and authors are technically comfortable
- Team already knows React/Vue components and wants to reuse them
- Budget allows higher cost per page (custom design = custom code)

## Nothing is taboo

Hybrid: WordPress headless + Astro frontend. Works. More complex, more expensive, but gives you the best of both when a client needs admin UX and a developer wants performance/control. I did two projects this way in 2025 — both successful, both over budget. If you go this route, plan 30% buffer.
