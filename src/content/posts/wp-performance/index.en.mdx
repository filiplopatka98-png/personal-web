WordPress has a reputation for being a slow CMS. Deserved? Partly. But after 5 years of tuning WP sites I can say: WordPress can be fast, you just need to know what you're doing.

## Three things that almost always change the game

**1. Drop the page builder.** Elementor, Divi, WPBakery — they all generate excessive HTML/CSS/JS. Unless you're building for a non-technical client with 50 block types, custom Gutenberg blocks are dramatically faster.

**2. Cache aggressively.** WP Rocket or W3 Total Cache + page cache + object cache (Redis) + critical CSS. Slow queries go from 800 ms to 8 ms.

**3. Optimize images on upload.** ShortPixel automatically or Imagify. WebP/AVIF. Lazy load. Biggest performance wins in one step.

## What doesn't help as much as you think

- Better hosting without other measures (expensive, marginal gains)
- "Optimization plugins" with 50+ toggles (indirect, frequent regressions)
- Migrating to headless just for speed (complexity > gain for most projects)

In practice: a solid WP with these 3 things hits Lighthouse 90+ without a headless rewrite. If you want 95+ and clean control, consider Astro or Next — but not as a "WordPress fix," that's a different project.
